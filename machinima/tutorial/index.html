Super Mario 64 machinima - The Guide

Hey, you have probably heard of people like Supermarioglitchy4, MarioMario54321 and Starman3. What do they have in common? They, and many others, all make cool videos with a game called Super Mario 64. "How do they do it?", you may ask. Worry not, this guide will hopefully answer all of your questions and even teach you how to make your own!

Forewords from the Author

It might be hard to believe, but this is actually the second iteration of my guide. The original was a mere blog post on the now-defunct YTR Forums, and it was never even close to a finished state. This was years ago, though, and a lot of things have changed since then.

I originally started this project when I was still in middle school. The first time I found out about this subgenre of machinima was a year before, in 2010 I believe, coming across SMG4 and MM54321's videos by chance when browsing through YouTube. What I learned then was that videogames could work as a storytelling medium even if you weren't the original developer yourself. It turned out that all the information regarding how to make such videos myself was scarce at best, though; presumably because people already had experience with producing machinima with other games. Luckily, it wasn't exactly rocket science; in just a week, I had already made my first video. Of course, it was nowhere near the quality I had seen from the others, but it was a start. Slowly and surely, though, I would eventually learn more advanced techniques and tools to help me reach those goals.

Eventually, I came to realise that there still wasn't a single, easy-to-approach guide for the essentials - and a little more than that - to the average machinimist. That's when I decided I'd at least try to do something about it. So the work on this project started. It was never finished, but now with a more accessible approach I'm going to try this again. The guide will retain its original planned shape, with a few additions, and it will also look prettier... eventually. Please be patient, folks!

I'm a mere Jack of All Trades, after all.
- Diapolo10
